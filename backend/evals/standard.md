# AlphaAgent Evaluation Standard (v1.0)

## Purpose

This document defines the standard evaluation criteria used to assess AlphaAgent analysis quality.

The goal is not to determine whether an investment conclusion is "correct", but to evaluate whether the analysis is useful, evidence-based, balanced, and actionable for investors.

---

# Scoring Overview

Each evaluation case receives a score out of 10.

| Category            | Max Score |
| ------------------- | --------- |
| Question Answering  | 2         |
| Data Usage          | 2         |
| Risk Analysis       | 2         |
| Structure & Clarity | 2         |
| Actionability       | 2         |
| Total               | 10        |

---

# 1. Question Answering (0-2)

Measures whether the report directly addresses the user's request.

### Score 2

* Fully answers the user's question.
* Covers all major requested topics.
* Analysis remains focused on the original request.

### Score 1

* Partially answers the question.
* Misses one or more important aspects.
* Includes excessive unrelated discussion.

### Score 0

* Fails to answer the user's question.
* Report is largely irrelevant.

### Examples

Question:

"Analyze Nvidia's growth outlook and valuation risks."

Good:

* Discusses growth drivers.
* Discusses valuation concerns.
* Provides balanced assessment.

Poor:

* Only summarizes company history.
* Never discusses valuation.

---

# 2. Data Usage (0-2)

Measures whether the analysis is supported by concrete information.

### Score 2

* Uses multiple relevant data points.
* References financial metrics, trends, business performance, market conditions, or retrieved evidence.
* Conclusions are supported by evidence.

### Score 1

* Uses some supporting data.
* Analysis is partially evidence-based.

### Score 0

* Mostly generic statements.
* Little or no supporting evidence.

### Examples

Strong evidence:

* Revenue growth
* Operating margin
* Free cash flow
* Debt levels
* Market share trends
* Valuation metrics

Weak evidence:

* "The company is doing well."
* "Growth looks strong."

---

# 3. Risk Analysis (0-2)

Measures whether the report considers downside scenarios and uncertainty.

### Score 2

* Identifies meaningful risks.
* Explains why those risks matter.
* Provides balanced discussion.

### Score 1

* Mentions risks briefly.
* Limited depth.

### Score 0

* Ignores risks entirely.
* Purely bullish or promotional.

### Examples

Meaningful risks:

* Competitive pressure
* Valuation compression
* Regulatory challenges
* Margin deterioration
* Demand slowdown
* Execution risk

---

# 4. Structure & Clarity (0-2)

Measures readability and organization.

### Score 2

* Clear sections and headings.
* Logical flow.
* Easy to scan and understand.

### Score 1

* Understandable but somewhat disorganized.

### Score 0

* Difficult to follow.
* Poor formatting.
* Confusing structure.

### Characteristics of High Quality Reports

* Executive summary
* Key findings
* Risk section
* Conclusion

---

# 5. Actionability (0-2)

Measures whether the report helps an investor decide what to watch next.

### Score 2

* Provides clear monitoring points.
* Identifies key metrics or future developments.
* Gives useful next-step guidance.

### Score 1

* Some useful observations.
* Limited practical guidance.

### Score 0

* No actionable takeaway.
* Reader finishes without knowing what matters next.

### Examples

Strong actionability:

* Monitor cloud revenue growth.
* Watch gross margin trends.
* Track debt refinancing.
* Follow regulatory developments.

Weak actionability:

* "Investors should be careful."
* "The future is uncertain."

---

# Final Score Interpretation

| Score Range | Rating     |
| ----------- | ---------- |
| 9-10        | Excellent  |
| 7-8         | Good       |
| 5-6         | Acceptable |
| 3-4         | Weak       |
| 0-2         | Poor       |
